Easy Solutions!

You can improve your carbon footprint and save money by following these few steps.

Check out this DOE site for more info.
  • Turn your thermostat back 7°-10°F for 8 hours a day from its normal setting to save up to 10% annually on heating and cooling.
  • The smaller the difference between the indoor and outdoor temperatures, the lower your overall cooling bill will be.
  • In the summer, set your theromstat to warmer than comfortable while you're away and a solid 78°F only when you're at home.
  • In the winter, set your thermostat to 68°F while you're awake, and lower while you're asleep or not at home. In fact, turning the thermostat one degree lower in the winter can save you up to 3% on your heating bill.

Slowly adjusting your household temperatures to adhere to these suggested steps will limit the amount of carbon you produce, as well as limiting the amount of money you spend on heating and cooling.

Interested in learning more? Read up on EnergyHub's helpful articles about whether cold weather makes you warmer on the inside and how much one degree is worth.

What to keep in mind while searching for a new AC unit!

Most units will have a SEER Rating, or a Seasonal Energy Efficiency Ratio. The higher the number is, the more efficient the system is, so keep a look out for systems with higher ratings to do the world a favor.

Why should I go efficient?

  • Lower energy bills: These units have better parts and materials that make better use of energy, which lowers your usage rate and thus, your energy costs.
  • Improved environmental footprint: Using less energy can help reduce the overall energy use of your home, and decrease your carbon footprint in the big picture.
  • Increased cooling performance: Using an efficient unit will cool the same room down quicker than a less efficient model, which means you get more comfortable, faster.
  • Better quality: Models with higher efficiency are typically made with better parts, which means the unit may be quieter than others and will have to run less to cool the air.
  • Smarter features: ENERGY STAR® compliant window units must have “smarter” features like an energy-saving mode and automatic reminders to replace the filters, both of which can make a significant dent in the energy you use each year.
