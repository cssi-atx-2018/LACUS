Our Purpose

In today’s society, the sheer number of appliances to be had in a single household is overwhelming. At times, it’s easy to forget to look for the cheapest and most energy-saving devices, and even if one remembers, it’s an arduous and time-consuming process. On top of that, humanity’s energy consumption can be extreme and leave heavy impacts on the planet. The average household consumes about 11 thousand kilowatt-hours of energy annually. That translates to nearly up to $1500 spent on electricity alone, and it takes 10 acres of US forests to sequester that much carbon from the atmosphere. And this isn’t even accounting for the consumption of an average commercial cooling system. We plan on resolving this problem of lack of information about energy usage for household appliances -- specifically, air conditioning and central cooling systems -- by creating a web application that allows users to check consumption of devices in order to save time, money, and energy .

Impact

One of our main features includes the ability for users to compare their average temperature settings to that of their relative region and state. The app will produce comparison data in response to these entries, showing users how their temperature range stands. It will also provide a hyperlink to a separate solutions page, which we’ll get into soon. Another one of our primary features is the capability for users to perform comparisons between various air conditioning brands in order to find out which would best serve their interests, whether that be saving money or conserving energy. As the great Walmart would say, with this information, users will be able to figure out how to save money and live better! Last but not least, we will be providing a specialized solution based on the users input in our database. These solutions will show exactly how the user can be involved and become a safe energized consumer on their own. This application can save lots of resources for thousands of people and we are eager to hear your interests in our idea. .

The Best Thermostat Types and Settings

According to ENERGY STAR, a homeowner can save as much as 10% a year in heating and cooling costs by turning back thermostats 7 - 10
degrees Fahrenheit for eight hours a day. Though you can manually set your thermostat daily, automated thermostats that are programmable or “smart” help you achieve greater
energy savings, especially if you’re away from home.
